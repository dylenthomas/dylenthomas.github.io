I can't take credit for this project as if I came up with it. I was inspired to pursue this project by a YouTuber named [Alex Lab](https://www.youtube.com/@ALEXLAB). He made many hydrogen reactors with various designs and I wanted to replicate the "sandwhich" design he made. This design involves a pattern of electrode -> rubber seal -> metal -> rubber seal -> electrode and so on. While it was difficult to assemble it worked and was extremely fun to work on. This is not the first version I made though. The very first attempt was just a sealed pickle jar with a 9v battery connected to some bolts through a thick copper wire. Unfortunately I don't have any pictures, but it had a gas line out the top of the pickle jar. It was really fun to play around with so I decided to make my own design. 

My own design was made from a PVC pipe and had the electrodes connected to a screw-on cap that had an exhaust tube and connections for the electrodes. It was then lowered into the PVC tube and filled with water. The efficiency and throughput was terrible (unfortunately I don't have numbers) and I determined this was mostly due to the design. My design had a poor surface area to volume ratio for conduction not realizing that it should be higher than lower to allow for high amounts of water to be broken down into the desired gas.
